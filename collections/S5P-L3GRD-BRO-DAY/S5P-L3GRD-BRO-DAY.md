## Total Column BrO by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
The Total Column Bromine Monoxide (BrO) product provides measurements of bromine oxide in the total atmospheric column, from the surface to the top of the atmosphere. The product was developed for monitoring the daily global spatial and temporal distribution of this trace gas in the Earth's atmosphere.
Inorganic bromine plays an important role in atmospheric chemistry: BrO contributes to mid-latitude ozone loss and polar ozone depletion in the stratosphere. In the troposphere, BrO is released over sea ice covered regions every polar spring, driving chemical reactions that contribute to ozone depletion and mercury deposition. BrO can further be found over salt lakes and in volcanic plumes.

#### About the dataset
- The BrO total vertical column product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- The product was developed for monitoring the daily global spatial and temporal distribution of bromine oxide in the Earth's atmosphere.
- Only high-quality measurements (qa_value > 0.5) are used in the final gridded product.
- More information on the TROPOMI BrO measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://data-portal.s5p-pal.com/product-docs/bro/S5P-BIRA-PRF-BRO_v01.02.02_20240806.pdf).

#### Use cases
BrO measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring polar ozone depletion events driven by bromine chemistry over sea ice regions every polar spring.
- Detecting and tracking BrO enhancements from volcanic eruptions, salt lakes, and salt marshes.
- Studying mercury deposition processes in polar regions linked to reactive bromine chemistry.
