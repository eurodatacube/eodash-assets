## Volcanic SO₂ COBRA (7km) by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
The Volcanic SO₂ COBRA (7km) product provides measurements of sulfur dioxide primarily from volcanic sources, retrieved assuming the SO₂ is located in a layer at 7 km altitude in the atmosphere, which is typical for volcanic plumes injected into the free troposphere.
This product provides daily snapshots of volcanic degassing and eruption plumes, enabling monitoring of volcanic activity and the transport of sulfur-rich emissions over large distances.

#### About the dataset
- The Volcanic SO₂ COBRA (7km) product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard Copernicus ESA's [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P).
- This product uses the Covariance-Based Retrieval Algorithm (COBRA), implemented operationally in November 2024.
- The retrieval assumes SO₂ is located in a 7 km altitude layer, making it particularly suited for detecting elevated volcanic plumes.
- Note that SO₂ from potential anthropogenic sources has not been filtered out and may still appear in the data.
More information on the TROPOMI SO₂ measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-BIRA-PRF-SO2-Sentinel-5P-Sulphur-Dioxide-Readme-2025-2.11.pdf).

#### Use cases

The Volcanic SO₂ COBRA (7km) product is useful for:
- Monitoring volcanic eruptions and passive degassing activity globally.
- Tracking the transport and dispersion of volcanic SO₂ plumes.
- Supporting aviation safety by detecting sulfur-rich ash and gas clouds that may pose risks to aircraft.
- Complementing anthropogenic SO₂ products for a more complete picture of tropospheric sulfur dioxide sources.
