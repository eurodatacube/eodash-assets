## Tropospheric Column NO₂ by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
Nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) is a short-lived air pollutant produced primarily by human activity, combustion of fossil fuels from road traffic, shipping, power plants, and industrial facilities. Burning activities and wildfires also contribute significantly. NO₂ has a direct impact on human health and indirectly contributes to the formation of ozone and fine particles. Because NO₂ concentrations respond rapidly to changes in emissions, they serve as useful indicators of economic activity and the effectiveness of emission reduction policies.

## About the dataset
- The Tropospheric Column NO₂product is measured  by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- The product provides the tropospheric NO₂ column between the surface and the top of the troposphere, as well as stratospheric and total column data.
- Because TROPOMI cannot see through thick clouds, near-surface concentrations can only be retrieved under cloud-free or partly cloudy conditions.
- More information on the TROPOMI NO₂ measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-KNMI-PRF-NO2%20-%20Sentinel-5P%20Nitrogen%20Dioxide%20Level%202%20Product%20Readme%20File%202025%20-%202.8.pdf).

## Use cases
NO₂ measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring air quality and anthropogenic pollution from traffic, industry, and power generation at urban and regional scales.
- Tracking emission reductions from policy interventions, economic slowdowns, or lockdown measures.
- Detecting NO₂ from wildfires, biomass burning, and shipping lanes.
