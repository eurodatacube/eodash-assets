## Total Column NO₂ by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Monthly)
The maps show the concentration of nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) in the lowest kilometers of the atmosphere. Nitrogen oxides are mainly produced by human activity and the combustion of (fossil) fuels, such as road traffic, ships, power plants and other industrial facilities. Burning activities and wildfires also contribute significantly to the NO2 concentrations observed. NO2 can have a significant impact on human health, both directly and indirectly through the formation of ozone and small particles.


#### About the dataset
- The NO2 product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- More information on the TROPOMI NO₂ measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-KNMI-PRF-NO2%20-%20Sentinel-5P%20Nitrogen%20Dioxide%20Level%202%20Product%20Readme%20File%202025%20-%202.8.pdf).

#### Use cases
NO2 measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring large-scale patterns of anthropogenic pollution from traffic, industry, and power generation.
- Studying stratospheric NO₂ chemistry and its seasonal variability.
