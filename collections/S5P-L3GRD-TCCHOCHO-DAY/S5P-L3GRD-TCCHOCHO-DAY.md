## Total Column CHOCHO (Glyoxal) by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
Glyoxal (CHOCHO) is a short-lived trace gas produced primarily in the atmosphere as an intermediate product in the oxidation of non-methane volatile organic compounds (NMVOCs). It is also directly emitted from fire events and combustion processes. Natural sources and fires account for approximately 70% of glyoxal production, with human activities contributing around 30%. Due to its short atmospheric lifetime of only a few hours, elevated glyoxal concentrations are observed close to emission sources, making it a valuable tracer for NMVOC emissions. Glyoxal also contributes significantly to the formation of secondary organic aerosols, impacting both air quality and climate forcing.

#### About the dataset
- The CHOCHO product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- Because glyoxal is a weak absorber, averaging of multiple observations in time and/or space is generally required to detect glyoxal signal reliably above the noise level. Only in extreme fire events may single-observation columns occasionally exceed the detection limit.
- More information on the TROPOMI CHOCHO measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product User Manual](https://data-portal.s5p-pal.com/product-docs/chocho/S5P-BIRA-PUM-CHOCHO_1.1.pdf).

#### Use cases
CHOCHO measurements are particularly useful for:

- Monitoring NMVOC emissions from natural vegetation, fires, and anthropogenic sources.
- Supporting air quality studies.
- Studying fire emissions and biomass burning events at global scale.
