## Total Column CH₄ – WFMD (Daily)
The Total Column Methane (CH₄) – WFMD product provides measurements of the column-averaged dry air mixing ratio of methane (XCH₄) in the atmosphere using an alternative retrieval algorithm. 
Methane is a key anthropogenic greenhouse gas and an important tracer for emissions from fossil fuels, agriculture, and natural sources.

#### About the dataset
- The CH₄ WFMD product is derived from measurements by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- The data is processed using the University of Bremen’s Weighting Function Modified DOAS (WFM-DOAS) v2.0 algorithm.
- More information on the algorithm and data quality can be found in the Algorithm Theoretical Baseline Document and [Product Readme File](https://www.iup.uni-bremen.de/carbon_ghg/products/tropomi_wfmd/data/v20/pug_wfmd.pdf).

#### Use cases
CH₄ WFMD measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring methane emissions from anthropogenic sources such as fossil fuel extraction, agriculture, and waste.
- Complementing operational CH₄ products with an independent retrieval algorithm for validation and comparison.
- Studying spatial and temporal variability of methane concentrations.
- Supporting climate research and greenhouse gas assessments.
