## Kd Ocean Colour UVA by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
The Kd Ocean Colour (UVA) product provides the diffuse attenuation coefficient (Kd) in the UVA wavelength range (356.5–390 nm), quantifying how sunlight is absorbed and scattered in the upper ocean layer in the ultraviolet-A spectral region. Data are essential for monitoring ocean optical properties, phytoplankton, and marine ecosystem health in the UV range.

#### About the dataset

- Kd-UVA measures the attenuation of downwelling plane irradiance in the ocean's first optical depth in the UVA wavelength range (356.5–390 nm).
- The product is generated using Differential Optical Absorption Spectroscopy (DOAS) adapted to oceanic applications (PhytoDOAS), with retrievals converted into physical Kd values using radiative transfer look-up tables. Only high-quality measurements (qa_value == 1.0) are used in the final gridded product.
More information on the TROPOMI Kd Ocean Colour measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://data-portal.s5p-pal.com/product-docs/kd/S5P_KD_PAL_PUM_v1.1.pdf).

#### Use cases
Kd-UVA measurements are particularly useful for:

- Studying light penetration and ocean color dynamics in the UVA wavelength range.
- Ocean biogeochemistry and primary productivity modeling.
- Monitoring phytoplankton blooms and water quality.
- Climate studies related to ocean radiative transfer and carbon cycling.
