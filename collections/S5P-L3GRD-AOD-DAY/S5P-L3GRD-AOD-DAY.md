## Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD) by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)

The Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD), also referred to as **Aerosol Optical Thickness (AOT)**, quantifies the **total column amount of aerosols by measuring how strongly particles scatter and absorb sunlight** between the surface and the top of the atmosphere.

Unlike [aerosol height products](https://eodashboard.org/explore/?x=0.0000&y=0.0000&z=2.3432&template=expert&indicator=S5P-L3GRD-ALH-DAY&datetime=2025-01-06), AOD does not provide vertical information. Instead, it indicates the overall abundance of aerosols in the atmospheric column: higher AOD values correspond to hazier conditions and larger aerosol burdens, while low values indicate relatively clean air.

#### About the dataset

- The AOD product is derived from measurements by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- It provides daily global spatial distribution of aerosol optical thickness across cloud-free scenes.
- The [retrieval algorithm](https://data-portal.s5p-pal.com/product-docs/aot/s5p_aot_pum_v2.1.0_signed.pdf) derives aerosol optical thickness in the 340–494 nm wavelength range, selected because it contains important information on aerosol absorption.
- The dataset captures the spatial distribution of aerosols from both natural sources (e.g. desert dust, volcanic ash, sea salt) and anthropogenic sources (e.g. industrial pollution, biomass burning).
- Together with the Sentinel-5P [Aerosol Absorbing Index (AAI)](https://eodashboard.org/explore/?x=0.0000&y=0.0000&z=2.4959&template=expert&indicator=S5P-L3GRD-AAI-DAY&datetime=2024-01-24) and [Aerosol Layer Height (ALH)](https://eodashboard.org/explore/?x=15.0000&y=18.3149&z=2.3450&template=expert&indicator=S5P-L3GRD-ALH-DAY&datetime=2025-01-06) products, AOD forms part of the Copernicus global aerosol monitoring suite.

#### Use cases

AOD is particularly useful for identifying large-scale aerosol events and assessing their evolution over time.Applications include:
- Air quality assessment and pollution monitoring
- Tracking desert dust and wildfire smoke transport
- Volcanic ash monitoring
- Climate research, including aerosol radiative forcing studies
- Validation and assimilation in atmospheric and transport models
