## Total Column O₃ by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
Ozone (O₃) plays a crucial role in the Earth's atmosphere. In the stratosphere, it forms the ozone layer, which protects life on Earth by absorbing harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the Sun. In the troposphere, ozone is a secondary pollutant that contributes to air quality degradation and can be harmful to human health, ecosystems, and vegetation at elevated concentrations. Ozone also acts as a greenhouse gas, influencing the Earth's radiative balance.

#### About the dataset
- Total column O₃ product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- The product provides the total ozone vertical column (ozone_total_vertical_column) between the surface and the top of the atmosphere, providing daily global coverage of the total atmospheric ozone column from the surface to the top of the atmosphere.
- More information on the TROPOMI O₃ measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-BIRA-PRF-O3-OFFL%20-%20Sentinel-5P%20OFFL%20Total%20Ozone%20Readme%202025%20-%202.10.pdf).

#### Use cases
Total column O₃ measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring stratospheric ozone and tracking the recovery of the ozone layer following the reduction of ozone-depleting substances.
- Detecting polar ozone depletion events such as the Antarctic ozone hole.
- Studying tropospheric ozone as an air quality indicator and greenhouse gas.
- Supporting UV radiation forecasting and health impact assessments.
