## Total Column CH₄ by Sentinel-5P TROPOMI (Daily)
The Total Column Methane (CH₄) product provides measurements of the column-averaged dry air mixing ratio of methane (XCH₄) in the atmosphere. Methane is the second most important anthropogenic greenhouse gas and a key indicator for monitoring fossil fuel extraction, agriculture, and wetland emissions.

#### About the dataset
- The CH₄ product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
- The product provides the column-averaged dry air mixing ratio of methane as well as a bias-corrected version.
- The data file contains the column average dry air mixing ratio of methane, XCH₄, which gives the total atmospheric column between the surface and the top of the atmosphere normalized to the corresponding dry air column. It also contains the data corrected for the XCH4 dependence on surface albedo.
- Only high-quality measurements (qa_value > 0.5) are used. Data gaps are visible due to quality filtering and the fact that over the sea only sun-glint measurements are provided.
- More information on the TROPOMI CH₄ measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-SRON-PRF-CH4%20-%20Sentinel-5P%20Methane%20Product%20Readme%20File%202025%20-%202.8.pdf).

#### Use cases
CH₄ measurements are particularly useful for:
- Monitoring anthropogenic methane emissions from fossil fuel extraction, agriculture, and waste management.
- Detecting and quantifying emission plumes from point sources such as oil and gas facilities.
- Studying wetland and natural methane sources.
- Climate research: methane is a potent greenhouse gas with significant radiative forcing.
