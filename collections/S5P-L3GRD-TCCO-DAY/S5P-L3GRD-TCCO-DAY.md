## Sentinel-5P Total Column CO (Daily)
The Sentinel-5P Total Column CO product provides measurements of carbon monoxide (CO) in the atmosphere. CO is a colorless, odorless, and toxic gas found in the atmosphere at low concentrations, in the troposphere, but higher near pollution sources. Major sources include fossil fuel combustion (vehicles), biomass burning, and vegetation. It is also produced through natural processes, such as volcanic activity and the oxidation of methane.

This product is important because carbon monoxide is a toxic gas that affects human health and acts as a tracer for air pollution transport across regions. CO is a trace gas in the atmosphere, and it does not have a direct effect on the global temperature, like methane and carbon dioxide do. However, carbon monoxide plays a major role in atmospheric chemistry, and it affects the ability of the atmosphere to cleanse itself of many other polluting gases. In combination with other pollutants and sunshine, it also takes part in the formation of lower-atmospheric (“bad”) ozone and urban smog.

#### About the dataset
- The CO product is measured by the [TROPOMI instrument](https://www.tropomi.eu/) aboard ESA’s [Sentinel-5P Precursor](https://www.esa.int/Applications/Observing_the_Earth/Copernicus/Sentinel-5P) satellite.
This dataset shows total column CO concentrations averaged over a 3-day period to reduce noise and data gaps.
CO has a lifetime in the atmosphere of about one month, allowing for monitoring of regional and transboundary transport of air pollution.
More information on the TROPOMI CO measurements and quality assessment can be found in the [Product Readme file](https://sentiwiki.copernicus.eu/__attachments/1673595/S5P-MPC-SRON-PRF-CO%20-%20Sentinel-5P%20Carbon%20Monoxide%20Level%202%20Product%20Readme%20File%202025%20-%202.9.pdf).

#### Use cases
The Sentinel-5P CO product is useful for:
- Monitoring anthropogenic and biomass burning CO emissions.
- Air quality assessment and pollution modeling.
- Tracking transport and dispersion of carbon monoxide plumes.
- Research on atmospheric chemistry and interactions with greenhouse gases.
